Mumbai

Mumbai, often called the "City of Dreams," is the financial, commercial, and entertainment capital of India. Situated on the western coast along the Arabian Sea, it is the most populous city in the country. The city is known for its fast-paced lifestyle, iconic skyline, and as the headquarters of major financial institutions such as the Bombay Stock Exchange and the Reserve Bank of India. From luxury skyscrapers to densely packed chawls, Mumbai represents the sharp contrasts of modern urban India.

Culturally, Mumbai is a melting pot of communities, languages, and traditions. It is famous worldwide for Bollywood, the Hindi film industry, which has given the city a unique glamour. Along with this, Mumbai hosts a vibrant theater scene, art exhibitions, and music festivals. The city is also renowned for its street food, with vada pav, pav bhaji, and bhel puri being local favorites. Festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi are celebrated on a grand scale, where the entire city comes together in devotion and festivity.

Apart from being a hub of finance and culture, Mumbai is also a city of landmarks and history. The Gateway of India, Marine Drive, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus, and Elephanta Caves are among its most famous attractions.
